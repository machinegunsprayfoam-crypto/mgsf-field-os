# Business Command Center Blueprint

## Goal

Turn Machine Gun Spray Foam & Concrete Lifting LLC into a controlled, repeatable, automation-ready operation.

## Main business lanes

1. Residential and commercial spray foam insulation
2. Commercial SPF roofing and roof coatings
3. Concrete lifting, leveling, void filling, and soil stabilization
4. Polyurea coatings
5. Building performance and energy upgrade work
6. Government and subcontracting opportunities

## Daily command workflow

1. Check new leads.
2. Qualify each lead.
3. Request missing project details/photos.
4. Create estimate.
5. Send proposal.
6. Follow up.
7. Schedule job.
8. Capture before/during/after photos.
9. Invoice and collect.
10. Store job closeout package.
11. Ask for review/referral.

## Lead qualification questions

- Customer name
- Phone
- Email
- Property address
- Service needed
- Residential, commercial, agricultural, industrial, or government
- Approximate square footage or dimensions
- Desired thickness or performance goal
- Access conditions
- Timeline and urgency
- Budget range, if appropriate
- Photos or drawings available
- Decision maker
- Competing bids, if any

## Estimating assumptions to capture

- Square footage
- Thickness
- Board feet
- Material yield
- Waste factor
- Consumables
- Labor hours
- Travel
- Mobilization
- Equipment needs
- Lift/scaffold needs
- Coating or ignition/thermal barrier requirements
- Margin
- Taxes/fees, if applicable

## Closeout package

- Signed proposal/contract
- Change orders
- Before photos
- During photos
- After photos
- Material batch/lot notes when available
- Invoice
- Payment record
- Warranty or maintenance notes
- Customer review request
